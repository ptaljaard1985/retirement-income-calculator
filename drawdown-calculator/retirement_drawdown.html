The calculator projects each year independently from each spouse’s starting age to the youngest spouse’s age 100, applying SARS 2026/27 income tax with bracket creep, CGT on discretionary gains, and the legislated 2.5%–17.5% living-annuity withdrawal band. LA draws escalate in rand terms at CPI each year, clamped into the band. Discretionary draws trigger CGT on the realised gain portion (proportional to base cost), included at 40% into taxable income with a R 50 000 annual exclusion per person. Tax is computed per spouse; auto-top-up solves a three-phase apportionment to meet the lifestyle target after tax.

Notes on methodology. Projections are annual. Year 1 living annuity income is calculated as the client's initial drawdown rate applied to the starting LA balance. In every subsequent year, the rand income escalates at the general inflation (CPI) rate, maintaining purchasing power. The resulting rand amount is expressed as a percentage of the LA balance at the start of that year and clamped to the legislated 2.5%–17.5% band if it falls outside. Draws that hit the 17.5% ceiling or are pushed up to the 2.5% floor are flagged in the projection table. LA income is fully taxable at marginal income-tax rates. Discretionary withdrawals are drawn down proportionally against the current balance, with the gain portion calculated as draw × (1 − base cost / balance); base cost is reduced pro-rata as capital is drawn. Capital gains tax is calculated per individual at the 2026/27 SARS framework: annual exclusion of R50 000, 40% inclusion rate, assessed at the individual's marginal rate (maximum effective 18%). Income tax rebates applied: primary R17 820, secondary R9 765 (age 65+), tertiary R3 249 (age 75+). Tax brackets, rebates, and the CGT annual exclusion are assumed to grow at 3% p.a. (approximation of SARS fiscal drag relief). When the auto-top-up option is enabled, the projection applies each year in three phases: (1) draw LA income at the CPI-escalated target; (2) if there is an after-tax shortfall against the household expense target, draw from each spouse's discretionary portfolio in proportion to their current balance (CGT on gains included in the solve); (3) if discretionary is exhausted and the gap remains, boost each spouse's LA draw in proportion to their LA balance, up to the 17.5% legal ceiling. Any residual shortfall after LA draws reach the ceiling is surfaced as a negative gap in the projection table. Future capital events (property sales, inheritances, maturing policies) are entered in today's money and inflated to nominal at the CPI rate; each event lands in the specified spouse's discretionary portfolio at the end of its designated year, with the full amount added to base cost so no unrealised gain is assumed on arrival. Other taxable income streams (rental income, DB pensions, trust distributions, maintenance orders) are entered as annual rand amounts in today's money; streams flagged as CPI-escalating grow each year at the general inflation rate while active, while flat streams stay fixed in nominal terms (and erode in real terms). Each stream contributes fully to taxable income at the relevant spouse's marginal rate while the spouse's age lies within the stated window. Values labelled "real" are deflated by CPI to express future amounts in current purchasing power. No allowance has been made for platform fees, advice fees, dividend withholding tax, interest exemptions, or medical tax credits; where relevant these should be factored in separately.
This projection is illustrative only and is based on the assumptions shown above as entered at the time of the meeting. Tax calculations use the 2026/27 SARS framework as announced in the February 2026 Budget and may be varied by subsequent legislation. Actual outcomes will differ, potentially materially, depending on investment performance, inflation, drawdown elections, tax changes, and individual circumstances.
